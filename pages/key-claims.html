Claim 1

Alleged Private Investor and Efforce Blockchain Profits at Retail Expense

Unrebutted

Efforce’s Main Private Investor (MPI), Anchor Investor, and treasury wallet (ETW) allegedly collectively profited over $400M through blockchain transactions, including a coordinated October 2021 dump, at the expense of retail token holders who suffered ~$400M in losses. The potential use of HBTC’s cold wallets allegedly enabled untraceable intra-exchange trading, obscuring ownership changes and amplifying harm.

1A — Alleged Main Private Investor (MPI) Blockchain Profits

The MPI wallet (0x078f...) received 450M WOZX on Dec 15th 2019 from the ETW (0x9d9e...), transferring 99.999M WOZX to the Anchor Investor (0xd5f...) on Dec 1st 2020. From Dec 2020 to Jan 2022, the MPI allegedly executed 58 outgoing transactions totaling 449.999M WOZX (~$283M), with 17.2M WOZX (~$30M at $1.74) pre-listing and ~432.8M WOZX (~$253M) post-listing. Key transfers included:

  • Pre-Listing Breaches: 17.2M WOZX (~$30M), violating the 0.27% daily vesting (1.215M WOZX/day) starting Dec 3rd 2020.
  • October 2021 Dump: 186.6M WOZX (~$69.2M, Oct 29th 2021), breaching vesting by 16.8M WOZX (~$8.2M).

Alleged total MPI profits (~$283M) were realized through sales to retail via HBTC, with prices dropping from $3.15 (Dec 9th 2020) to $0.37 (Oct 29th 2021, CoinGecko).

1B — Alleged Anchor Investor Blockchain Profits

The Anchor Investor wallet (0xd5f...) allegedly received 99.999M WOZX (~$174M at $1.74) from the MPI on Dec 1st 2020. From Dec 2020 to Oct 2021, it transferred ~71.2M WOZX (~$53.2M), all compliant with the 0.27% vesting.

  • Alleged October 2021 Dump: 71.2M WOZX (~$34.8M at $0.49, Oct 6th 2021) to 0x3ad5d910..., coinciding with HBTC’s closure.

1C — Alleged Efforce Treasury Wallet (ETW) Blockchain Profits

The ETW (0x9d9e...) held 550M WOZX (200M Team, 200M Mining, 150M Consulting) post-private sale. From Nov 29th 2020 to Mar 15th 2023, it executed 54 transactions transferring 173.3M WOZX (~$68.1M), with only 13.1M WOZX (~$2.6M) accounted for (staking, competitions). Unaccounted transfers: 160.2M WOZX (~$68.1M).

  • Pre-Listing Breach: 25M WOZX (~$43.9M at $1.74, Dec 2nd 2020, tx: 0x910179...) to Wallet 1 (0xEEbC...).
  • October 2022 Transfer: 75M WOZX (~$7.9M at $0.11, Oct 25th 2022, tx: 0x1b71f9...) to Wallet 3 (0x697D...), labeled “security” but admitted privately as a U.S. VC sale.

1D — Alleged Verifiable Retail Losses

Retail losses estimated at minimum ~$400M, reflecting the combined alleged profits of MPI (~$283M), Anchor Investor (~$53.2M), and ETW (~$68.1M). WOZX dropped from $2.88 (Dec 2020) to $0.0024 (Nov 2025).

1E — Alleged Hot Wallet Theory and HBTC’s Role

The Hot Wallet Theory posits that wallets involved in the October 2021 dump may have been cold wallets within HBTC, with intra-exchange trading allegedly obscuring ownership changes until withdrawals. HBTC’s lax KYC/AML (Forbes, Jul 6th 2020) allegedly allowed MPI, Anchor, and ETW to trade internally.

Claim 6

Non-Delivery of Platform and Utility

Unrebutted

Efforce failed to deliver its promised blockchain platform, MWOZ credits, staking rewards, and NFT-based governance, rendering WOZX valueless. Alleged misleading promises and early closure of staking pools contributed to ~$400M in losses.

6A — Alleged Failure to Deliver Blockchain Platform

Whitepaper promised a blockchain platform for tokenizing energy savings. Castiglione claimed Q1 2021 launch. Mar 20th 2021 beta lacked projects, MWOZ, or functionality. Platform scrapped Q1 2022 for NFT iteration, also failed. Visetti admitted: “No blockchain was needed” (Nov 18th 2023). As of Nov 2025, efforce.io shows a pivot to environmental commodities, no blockchain or MWOZ.

6B — Alleged Genesis Pool Failures

Genesis Pool launched May 20th 2021, raised ~$600K (~$500K seeded by Efforce, $100K from contributors). No platform integration or rewards materialized. Contributors waited until Sep 2023 for refunds with nominal 10% ROI. Visetti: “We paid $50k in cancellation fees [to 80+ ESCOs]” (Jul 2024).

6C — Alleged Misleading Staking APR

Staking launched Mar 2022 with 13.1M WOZX, promising 2168.6% APR. Pools closed early Sep 2022, refunding 3.7M WOZX ($50,134.07, Mar 2024) at 10% ROI. 3–4M WOZX remain unaccounted.

6D — Alleged Unfulfilled Governance Promises

NFT-based voting promised 2021–2022, deemed unviable by 2023. Bithumb delisting response promised WOZX would become governance token — no mechanisms implemented. Refunds delayed until Aug 2023.
