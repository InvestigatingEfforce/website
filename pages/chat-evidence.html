General Holders — Team False Claims vs Official Records

The Efforce Discord server’s General Holders channel (Mar 2022 – Dec 2024) contained 4,661 messages from 81 users. Two team accounts were active: staylamb (WizX / Efforce community manager, also used by Paolo Pastore) with 529 messages, and jackaither (Jacopo Visetti) with just 17 messages. Below are the most significant team claims that are directly contradicted by official company filings.

#1
“We are probably among the few companies being profitable in this sector.”
Jacopo Visetti (jackaither) — General Holders — Aug 10, 2022
Reality: Malta filings: €342K loss (2021), €0 revenue (2023). No year on record shows profitability. This is a public false statement to token holders in a channel where trading decisions were being discussed.
#2
“We never had so many requests for project financing as now.”
Jacopo Visetti (jackaither) — General Holders — Aug 10, 2022
Reality: Zero projects were ever onboarded on the Efforce platform. Zero revenue from energy efficiency was recorded in any filing. Zero customers appear in any official accounts.
#3
“The company is sound and we should be able to communicate some good news regarding the energy efficiency projects developed so far.”
Jacopo Visetti (jackaither) — General Holders — Apr 22, 2023
Reality: Filed losses, zero employees, zero revenue. The “good news” was never communicated. No energy efficiency projects were ever disclosed to token holders.
#4
“We are in no way short on cash and are nowhere near the end of our cash runway.”
Jacopo Visetti (jackaither) — General Holders — Apr 22, 2023
Reality: Malta filings show minimal recorded activity. If the cash runway was long, the cash source is unexplained given €0 platform revenue and no disclosed private investor funds in accounts.
#5
“EFFORCE actually underwent a global Due Diligence by Bithumb last year and the Token was reconfirmed by them.”
Jacopo Visetti (jackaither) — General Holders — Apr 22, 2023
Reality: Bithumb delisted WOZX in November 2023 — just 7 months after Visetti claimed the token had been “reconfirmed.” The team repeatedly told holders “don’t be afraid” and “we see no reason to be delisted” right up until the delisting occurred.
#6
“We have industrial customers we will disclose as soon as we release the platform.”
staylamb (WizX/Efforce) — General Holders — Mar 21, 2022
Reality: No industrial customers were ever disclosed. Zero customers appear in any official filing. The platform launched in June 2024 with no customer announcements.
#7
“We have agreements and common strategies to make the business grow and to not hurt small investors.”
staylamb (WizX/Efforce) — General Holders — Jun 13, 2022
Reality: The Major Private Investor (MPI) dumped tokens generating an estimated $283M in profit. Retail holders lost an estimated $300M+ collectively. The “agreements to not hurt small investors” either did not exist or were systematically ignored.
#8
“It’s not a problem since we just had a serious audit by them. Company is sound.”
staylamb (WizX/Efforce) — General Holders — Apr 24, 2023
Reality: No audit was ever published. The auditing entity was never identified publicly. As of 2025, no 2022 or 2023 accounts have been filed for Efforce Ltd (Malta).
#9
“Token holders are not investors, are just token holders. Tokens are not stocks and don’t represent stakes of the company.”
staylamb (WizX/Efforce) — General Holders — Dec 21, 2022
Reality: Token holders purchased WOZX after Steve Wozniak stated “I see owning WOZX like owning stock.” The whitepaper promised utility, governance, and a share of savings. The team’s own buyback promises (2023) contradict the claim that holders have no stake. This statement was made while warning a community member for asking to hear from Visetti directly.

Five Key Patterns

Pattern 1: Perpetual “Soon”

The staylamb account used the word “soon” 40 times across the General Holders chat. Promises that never materialised: platform launch, marketing campaign, updated whitepaper, new exchange listings, governance features, buyback programme.

Pattern 2: Shoot the Messenger

Users were warned, banned, or had accounts restricted for asking hard questions. Examples: “This attitude is not welcome. You are now a warned member” (Dec 2022, to a user who simply asked to hear from Visetti). “Please mind your words. We will ban whoever doesn’t follow community guidelines” (Jun 2024, to a user who said the community was dead). Copley’s account was restricted after posting the 9-point questionnaire.

Pattern 3: Deflection Over Substance

When confronted with specific questions about wallets and tokens: “The blockchain is transparent, you can check.” When pressed on unanswered questions: “We keep answering the same questions.” When asked about revenue: “The platform is not yet profitable… it is simple as that.”

Pattern 4: Bithumb Cycle (3 Warnings)

Warning 1 (Jun 2022): “We will resolve.” → Resolved. Warning 2 (Apr 2023): “No petition to delist.” → Resolved. Warning 3 (Nov 2023): “We see no reason to be delisted.” → DELISTED. Each cycle, community was told not to worry, then gaslit when the worst outcome occurred: “Bithumb unilaterally decided to delist, we had no say.”

Pattern 5: Progressive Stripping of Token Utility

2020 (Whitepaper): Governance, savings share, platform access, staking. 2022: Governance “coming soon,” savings via NFTs, staking rewards. 2023: NFTs cancelled/refunded, staking removed, governance shelved, buyback promised. 2024: Buyback never executed, zero utility remaining, whitepaper removed (404). Result: Token went from multi-utility to zero utility while team retained 537M tokens.
