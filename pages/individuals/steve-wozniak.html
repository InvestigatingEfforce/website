Introduction

Efforce, a blockchain-based energy efficiency platform outlined as co-founded by Steve Wozniak, promised a decentralized solution but failed to deliver, resulting in ~$350M in retail investor losses by June 2025. Blockchain evidence, insider admissions, and whistleblower allegations reveal a pattern of fraud, misrepresentation, and regulatory violations, with Wozniak’s high-profile endorsements amplifying the scheme. The WOZX token, named directly after Wozniak, and an NFT iteration launched in 2022 featuring his caricature leveraged his fame to drive perceived value and rarity, despite the project’s collapse in 2023. As an 11% shareholder, Wozniak’s actions and inactions implicate him in criminal and civil charges under U.S., Maltese, and EU jurisdictions. This article outlines his liability, supported by transaction data, misleading statements, and legal precedents.

Wozniak’s Role and Alleged Misrepresentations

Wozniak’s prominence as an Apple co-founder drove Efforce’s credibility, inflating WOZX token prices to $2.88 (Dec 2020, FXStreet) before crashing to $0.0033 (Jun 2025, CoinGecko). The token, named WOZX in his honor, and an NFT iteration launched in 2022 with “Steve Wozniak” caricature images, capitalized on his association to create perceived rarity and value, though the NFT “reveal” never occurred as the project was scrapped in 2023 (Section 9, Nov 18, 2023, Call Summary). His key actions include:

  • “Shares of Stock” Claim: On Yahoo Finance (Dec 8, 2020), Wozniak stated, “I think of owning WOZX as shares of stock,” misleading investors about token utility, as WOZX lacked equity rights or platform functionality (Section 2A, Exposé).
  • Decentralized Platform Hype: In a Businesswire press release (Dec 14, 2020), Wozniak claimed Efforce was a “decentralized platform,” despite no platform materializing by Jun 2025. Jacopo Visetti admitted decentralization was unfeasible (Nov 18, 2023, Section 2C, Exposé). In reality all WOZX tokens sold via private sale were transferred to an anonymous single US-based investor/entity who controlled the market in it's entirety on day one of Exchange listing via it's own holding and it's subsequently onboarded affiliate network.
  • U.S.-Targeted Marketing: Wozniak’s endorsements, including a Times Square billboard (Dec 2020), targeted U.S. investors, despite WOZX restrictions for U.S. persons (Whitepaper, Page 10, $68M Report), establishing SEC jurisdiction.
  • Failure to Correct Misstatements: Wozniak ignored 20+ outreach attempts by whistleblower Michael Copley (2023–2025, Section 2B, Exposé) regarding ~$68M unaccounted from Efforce wallets (Nov 2020–Mar 2023, $68M Report) and the Main Private Investor’s (MPI) ~$350M dump (Section 1, Exposé). He also failed to address Efforce’s false Bithumb claim of his platform launch (Jun 23, 2022, Section 8F, Exposé).
  • Undisclosed Internal Investigation: Wozniak allegedly participated in a private investigation into private investor misconduct, confirmed by Visetti (Nov 18, 2023, Call Summary, Section 1), which found no proof of wrongdoing despite MPI’s 55.3M WOZX vesting breaches (~$296M, 450m Report). This investigation was never disclosed to token holders, concealing potential market manipulation.

Evidence: Blockchain data shows the MPI (0x078f4ffc...) dumped ~$336.2M (450M WOZX, Section 1, Exposé), with 173.3M WOZX (~$68.1M) unaccounted from the Efforce Treasury Wallet (ETW, $68M Report). Wozniak’s statements fueled a $1.6B valuation peak (Dec 2020, Section 3B, Exposé), enabling these profits.

Possible Criminal Charges

Fraud by False Representation

Wozniak’s “shares of stock” claim and decentralized platform hype, amplified by the WOZX naming and NFT caricature, constitute fraud under 18 U.S.C. § 1343 (wire fraud). His statements misled investors expecting a functional platform, which-Visetti admitted was undelivered (Section 6A, Exposé). Blockchain evidence of 17.2M WOZX (~$30M) pre-listing transfers (Dec 1–3, 2020, tx: 0x9d04f5..., 450m Report) and Seele’s alleged Ponzi trades (Nov 16, 2023, Section 13A, Exposé) show market manipulation Wozniak’s endorsements facilitated.

Precedent: SEC v. Ripple Labs, Inc. (2023) established liability for misleading crypto promotions.

Aiding and Abetting Securities Fraud

Wozniak’s failure to correct Efforce’s misrepresentations, including his co-founder status, the MPI’s ~$283M dump (Section 1A, Exposé) and ~$68M unaccounted ETW transfers (Nov 2020–Mar 2023, $68M Report), aided securities fraud under SEC Rule 10b-5 (15 U.S.C. § 78j(b)). His prominence enabled a U.S.-based PI to profit, confirmed by Pastore’s pump-and-dump admission (Nov 15, 2022, Section 11A, Exposé). The California lawsuit (25CV459519, Feb 27, 2025, Section 7A, Exposé) and U.S. probes (Visetti, Nov 18, 2023, Section 7A, Exposé) reinforce SEC jurisdiction. 

Precedent: Matrixx Initiatives, Inc. v. Siracusano (563 U.S. 27, 2011) upheld liability for omissions of material facts.

Civil Charges

Negligent Misrepresentation

Wozniak’s technical expertise obligated him to verify Efforce’s claims. His silence on ~$68M unaccounted from ETW wallets, the MPI’s ~$350M dump, and Efforce’s non-delivery, despite Copley’s outreach, reflects negligence under California Civil Code § 1710. Efforce’s false Bithumb claim (Jun 23, 2022) and ZD Assurance’s resignation over wallet transparency (2022, Section 7B, Exposé) highlight his due diligence failure.

Evidence: ETW’s 160.2M WOZX (~$68.1M) unaccounted transfers (Nov 2020–Mar 2023, $68M Report) and 25M WOZX pre-listing breach (Dec 2, 2020, tx: 0x910179..., $68M Report) show financial opacity Wozniak ignored.

Breach of Fiduciary Duty

As an 11% shareholder, Wozniak owed token holders a duty of care. Pastore’s admission that Wozniak “abandoned ship without doing much” (Aug 24, 2023, Section 12A, Exposé) and his inaction on the undisclosed internal investigation into PI misconduct (Nov 18, 2023, Call Summary) breached this duty. The lack of crypto activity in Efforce Ltd (Malta) filings (€25K revenue, Feb 12, 2025, $68M Report) further indicates oversight failures Wozniak permitted.

Precedent: Stone v. Ritter (911 A.2d 362, Del. 2006) established shareholder liability for oversight failures.

 

Jurisdictional Considerations

  • U.S. Jurisdiction: Wozniak’s California residency, Efforce’s Times Square billboard, and U.S. PI profits ($283M, Section 2C, Exposé) establish SEC and DOJ jurisdiction. The California lawsuit (25CV459519, Feb 27, 2025) and Visetti’s admission of U.S. probes (Nov 18, 2023) confirm scrutiny.
  • Malta and EU Jurisdiction: ZD Assurance’s resignation (2022, Section 7B, Exposé) and Visetti’s GDPR-violating intimidation of Copley (Apr 3, 2024, Section 12C, Exposé) implicate Malta’s VFA Act (Cap. 590) and EU’s MiFID II (Article 24). Eurojust coordinates investigations.
  • International Reach: Seele’s alleged Ponzi trades via HBTC (Nov 16, 2023, Section 13A, Exposé) and 0x3ad5d910...’s HBTC linkage (450m Report) expose Wozniak to AML violations (31 U.S.C. § 5318).

Systemic Misconduct Context

Wozniak’s liability is amplified by Efforce’s systemic issues:

  • Private Investor Dumping: The MPI dumped 186.6M WOZX (~$69.2M, Oct 29, 2021, tx: 0x5cef87..., 450m Report), breaching vesting schedules (16.8M WOZX excess, Section D, 450m Report). Pastore confirmed a pump-and-dump (Nov 15, 2022, 450m Report).
  • Treasury Wallet Breaches: ETW (0x9d9e8607...) transferred 173.3M WOZX (~$68.1M), with 160.2M unaccounted (Nov 2020–Mar 2023, $68M Report). A 25M WOZX pre-listing breach (Dec 2, 2020, tx: 0x910179...) diluted the market.
  • Financial Oversight Failures: Efforce Ltd (Malta) filings omit crypto activity (€25K revenue, Feb 12, 2025, $68M Report), and Efforce Ltd (Bahamas) filed no accounts, despite $12.5M–$36M private sale claims (Section 8, Call Summary).
  • Seele/HBTC Network: Seele’s WOZX promotion at GDAC 2020 (Nov 10, 2020, 450m Report) and HBTC’s lax controls (Forbes, Jul 6, 2020) enabled intra-exchange trading, obscured by 0x3ad5d910... (96.8M WOZX, $51.9M, 450m Report).
  • Intimidation Tactics: Visetti’s threats against Copley (Dec 16, 2023) and Pastore’s GDPR violation (Apr 3, 2024, Section 12C, Exposé) suppressed transparency, enabled by Wozniak’s disengagement.

Evidence: Cross-over through 0x3ad5d910... and 0x8e372e... (122.7M WOZX, ~$63M, 450m Report) shows coordinated dumping, with Efforce’s non-disclosure misleading investors.

Investor Impact

Wozniak’s endorsements, WOZX naming, and NFT caricature drove WOZX to $2.88 (Dec 2020, FXStreet), misleading retail investors who suffered ~$350M in losses by Jun 2025 (CoinGecko, $0.0033). The MPI’s 186.6M WOZX dump (~$69.2M, Oct 29, 2021, 450m Report) and ETW’s 160.2M WOZX unaccounted transfers (~$68.1M, $68M Report) fueled this harm, amplified by Wozniak’s failure to address financial opacity or PI misconduct, despite his involvement in the internal investigation.

Conclusion

Steve Wozniak’s endorsements, including his “shares of stock” claim (Dec 8, 2020), co-founder marketing (Dec 4, 2020), and the WOZX token and NFT caricature leveraging his fame, fueled Efforce’s fraudulent scheme, contributing to ~$350M in retail losses. His failure to address ~$68M unaccounted from Efforce wallets, the MPI’s ~$350M dump, and the undisclosed internal investigation into PI misconduct reflects negligence and breaches of fiduciary duty, actionable under U.S. (SEC Rule 10b-5), Maltese (VFA Act), and EU (MiFID II) laws. Despite claims of limited involvement, Wozniak’s prominence enabled Seele’s Ponzi trades and Visetti’s intimidation, per Pastore’s admissions (Nov 15, 2022). Immediate investigation into his communications, financial benefits, and awareness is essential to ensure accountability for token holders harmed by Efforce’s systemic misconduct. Regulatory action, including SEC complaints and potential DOJ wire fraud charges, is warranted.
