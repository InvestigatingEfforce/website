Stefano Scozzese is listed on Efforce’s own website and historical marketing materials as Head of Communication and Co-Founder. An award-winning Italian creative director, Scozzese was the architect of Efforce’s visual identity, branding, websites, whitepaper design, and much of the promotional material that presented the project as a revolutionary, Wozniak-backed, financially robust platform.

Despite holding no formal directorship (Malta Business Registry shows no record of him as a director or shareholder), Scozzese was intimately involved in crafting and disseminating the public narrative that induced hundreds of millions in retail investment. As the person responsible for the look, feel, and messaging of Efforce from 2018–2023, he is alleged to have knowingly or negligently participated in the dissemination of materially false and misleading representations while never once speaking up when those representations were proven false.

Scozzese’s credentials are significant because they demonstrate he was not a junior freelancer following instructions — he was a senior, award-winning creative professional listed as a co-founder who would have understood the impact and implications of the materials he designed and the claims they contained.

Alleged Criminal Charges

1. Aiding and Abetting Fraud by False Representation

Malta Criminal Code Art. 308; Italian Penal Code Art. 640

As Head of Communication and the creative force behind Efforce’s public image, Scozzese allegedly designed and published materials that repeatedly misrepresented:

  • AitherCO2 as the “mother company” with hundreds of millions in revenue;
  • Efforce as having 40–50 employees and thousands of clients inherited from AitherCO2;
  • Steve Wozniak as an active co-founder and engineer;
  • The platform as “days/weeks/months” from launch — for years;
  • Private investors as long-term Asian institutions when they were primarily a single U.S.-based entity that dumped aggressively.

2. Market Manipulation through Misleading Marketing

EU Market Abuse Regulation Art. 12; U.S. 18 U.S.C. § 1348

Alleged creation and dissemination of glossy promotional materials, websites, and social media assets that artificially inflated confidence in WOZX while treasury and private-investor dumping occurred in the background.

Alleged Civil Charges

  • Negligent Misrepresentation — designed and published dozens of false or misleading statements that induced investment and continued holding of WOZX, causing estimated retail losses exceeding $400M.
  • Aiding and Abetting Breach of Fiduciary Duty — knowingly provided the creative tools and public face that enabled directors and officers to breach their duties to token holders.
  • Unjust Enrichment — received payment (directly or via his agency) for branding and communication services while the project collapsed and retail investors were harmed.
  • Conspiracy / Concerted Action — worked in concert with Castiglione, Vanetti, Visetti, and others to present a unified false narrative over a multi-year period.

Jurisdictional Considerations

  • Italy: Residence, primary place of business, location of his creative agency.
  • Malta: Efforce Ltd registration; marketing targeted Maltese-regulated entity.
  • United States: California class-action lawsuit (25CV459519); U.S.-based PI; marketing reached U.S. citizens.
  • EU-wide: Market Abuse Regulation, Unfair Commercial Practices Directive.

The Man Who Built the Illusion — And Never Corrected It

Stefano Scozzese was not just a hired designer — he was a listed co-founder and the Head of Communication. He personally designed and maintained:

The efforce.io website that conflated AitherCO2 employees and clients as Efforce’s own for years.
Whitepaper and pitch-deck designs that presented AitherCO2 as the financially robust “parent company” — while it faced a €319.9M tax liability.
Promotional graphics claiming “40–50 employees,” “thousands of clients,” and imminent platform launches that never materialised — for a company with 0 employees per Malta filings.
Social media and PR assets that continued to portray Efforce as active and successful long after the platform was abandoned and treasury tokens vanished.
Brand identity and visual language that lent an air of institutional credibility and professionalism to what Malta filings reveal was a shell company with cumulative losses of €342,780.

Despite his central role in shaping public perception, Scozzese has never distanced himself from the false narrative, never issued corrections, and never spoken publicly about the collapse — even after AitherCO2’s €319.9M tax liability, the €510 stake sale, the missing treasury tokens, and the non-delivery of the platform became undeniable.

When the person who designs the brand knows (or should know) the brand is built on falsehoods, silence becomes complicity.

Conclusion

Stefano Scozzese occupied a unique position in the Efforce story: the award-winning creative professional who built the visual world that made investors believe. Every glossy graphic, every polished website page, every whitepaper layout was designed to project institutional credibility for a company that had 0 employees, no platform, and no revenue. He was listed as a co-founder and Head of Communication — not a passive freelancer but an active participant in crafting the narrative. The materials he designed were the primary vehicle through which false claims about AitherCO2’s financial health, team size, client base, and platform readiness reached investors. That he has never corrected, retracted, or distanced himself from any of it suggests either ongoing complicity or a calculated decision to remain silent. All allegations remain entirely unrebutted.
