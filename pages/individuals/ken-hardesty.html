Ken Hardesty is a California-based U.S. citizen, 4% shareholder and board member of Efforce, and CEO in Residence at Gerbsman Partners. According to Jacopo Visetti on the Nov 18, 2023 call, Hardesty was the person who “found the private investor” — the Main Private Investor (MPI) who received 450M WOZX and subsequently dumped 186.6M tokens for approximately $283M in profit, while retail investors lost everything.

Hardesty is also one of three individuals who held the Efforce crypto wallet keys (alongside Sergio Carloni and Jacopo Vanetti), giving him direct control over the 550M WOZX treasury. He is alleged to be one of the U.S.-based private investors referenced by Visetti as “One from NYC… Western US.”

His California residency and U.S. citizenship place him directly within SEC and DOJ jurisdiction — the most aggressive enforcement regime for securities fraud globally.

Alleged Criminal Charges

1. Alleged Securities Fraud (SEC Rule 10b-5)

Hardesty allegedly selected the MPI without adequate due diligence, failed to disclose material facts about the private placement, and benefited from insider knowledge:

  • MPI $283M dump — 186.6M WOZX sold by the investor Hardesty allegedly sourced (completed Oct 29, 2021).
  • Non-disclosure of vesting breaches — 55.3M WOZX released ahead of published schedule while Hardesty served on the board.
  • Alleged U.S. private investor — “One from NYC… Western US” (Visetti, Nov 18, 2023).
  • MPI selection — “Ken Hardesty… found the private investor” (Visetti, Nov 18, 2023).

Applicable laws: U.S. 18 U.S.C. § 1343 (wire fraud, up to 20 years), SEC Rule 10b-5 (fraud in connection with securities), Section 20(a) (control person liability).

2. Alleged Breach of Fiduciary Duty

As a 4% shareholder and board member, Hardesty owed fiduciary duties to token holders:

  • Wallet key control — direct ability to approve/block treasury transfers including the $68M+ unaccounted movements.
  • Board oversight failure — served on the Efforce board during the entire period of private investor misconduct and failed to intervene.
  • Gerbsman Dubai promotion (Dec 6, 2021) — Gerbsman Partners blog showcased an Efforce/Wozniak event as a firm initiative, potentially using the Gerbsman brand to legitimise the project.

Applicable laws: Delaware § 144 (if applicable), California Corporations Code § 25402 (insider trading / material non-public information), Malta Companies Act.

Alleged SEC Liability (U.S.)

Hardesty is the most directly exposed individual to U.S. securities enforcement due to his California residency and U.S. citizenship:

  • Howey Test — WOZX marketed as investment in Efforce’s energy efficiency platform with expected returns.
  • Rule 10b-5 — material omissions (true MPI identity, vesting breaches, treasury movements) in connection with securities.
  • Section 5 (Securities Act 1933) — involvement in unregistered offering of securities to U.S. investors, despite whitepaper prohibition.
  • Section 20(a) (Exchange Act) — control person liability as board member and wallet key holder.

Key factor: Unlike other Efforce principals based in Italy, Malta, or Switzerland, Hardesty is a U.S. citizen on U.S. soil. The SEC, DOJ, FINRA, and California DFPI all have direct, unambiguous jurisdiction. No extradition or international cooperation is required.
