All criminal and civil charges listed below are alleged and have not been proven in court. This page is published in the public interest under Defamation Act 2013 s.4.



Jacopo Vanetti, as the Chief Technology Officer (CTO) and 40% shareholder of Efforce Ltd (Malta), played a pivotal role in the technical and promotional efforts for the WOZX token. As the largest individual shareholder, he had direct oversight responsibility for major corporate decisions and representations. His actions — including unfulfilled platform promises, misleading statements, participation in an unofficial price discussion group, failure to enforce smart-contract vesting, and complicity in the concealment of AitherCO2’s financial distress and the nominal transfer of its 42.5% stake (potentially worth €600m+) for just £510 — have raised profound legal and ethical concerns. This updated document incorporates additional context from the Efforce Exposed article, detailed blockchain analyses (450M WOZX Private Investor Wallets, $68M Unexplained Transactions from Efforce 550M WOZX Treasury Wallet), and the Efforce & WOZX Timeline of Events, detailing potential criminal and civil charges supported by new evidence of misconduct, governance failures, and financial obfuscation. The allegations suggest Vanetti’s actions contributed to ~$400m+ in retail investor losses through a combination of fraud, market manipulation, and operational non-delivery. A comprehensive quote section at the end compiles key statements relevant to future investigations.

Alleged Criminal Charges

1. Alleged Fraud by False Representation

Description: Vanetti made or oversaw false or reckless statements about Efforce’s technological capabilities, financial backing, corporate structure, and vesting enforcement to induce investment in WOZX tokens.

Examples:

  • Platform Promises (Mar–Apr 2021): Vanetti claimed Efforce’s platform had a “secret” phase that would “change blockchain” and solve “all chains’ biggest issues” (Telegram, Mar 21, 2021). He also promised a “killer app” and DeFi protocol paper (Telegram, Apr 14, 2021). No such advancements were delivered by Nov 2025, with Visetti later admitting “no blockchain was needed” (Discord, Nov 18, 2023).
  • Exchange Listings (Mar–Apr 2021): Vanetti announced “four exchange listings in the coming days” (Telegram, Mar 31, 2021). Only Bitcoin.com was listed (Apr 21, 2021).
  • Personal Token Purchases & Hype (Dec 2020): Vanetti posted “WOZX $0.99 Price on the moon” and claimed to buy tokens as gifts (Telegram, Dec 3–8, 2020), without disclosing the MPI’s control of 450M WOZX or ETW’s 25M WOZX transfer (~$43.9M, tx: 0x910179..., Dec 2, 2020).
  • AitherCO2 Financial Misrepresentation (2020–2021): Efforce, under Vanetti’s oversight as 40% shareholder, repeatedly presented AitherCO2 as a financially robust “mother company” with revenues of $250m–$1.2B, while concealing its €319.9m tax liability (notified 2018, provisioned minimally, disclosed only Apr 2022) and €408.2m loss. Visetti claimed “~€350m turnover” at Community Gathering #6 (Jun 25, 2021); Vanetti was present and did not correct.
  • Corporate Structure & Stake Transfer Concealment: Vanetti oversaw a structure that allowed AitherCO2 to transfer its 42.5% stake in Efforce Ltd (Malta) — potentially worth €600m+ based on Dec 2020 token treasury valuation — to MBM Holdings AG for a nominal £510 (Dec 15, 2020), undisclosed to token holders until years later. No legal parent-subsidiary relationship existed despite public claims of AitherCO2 as “mother company”.
  • Private Investor Misrepresentation: Efforce falsely claimed the 450M WOZX private sale went to “Asia-based cornerstone institutional investors who will not sell”, when the entire allocation went to a single US-based Main Private Investor (MPI) plus US-linked anchor who sold aggressively.
  • Vesting Misrepresentation: Vanetti supported claims of strict vesting (e.g., 20-year team vesting, Jun 25, 2021), despite no smart-contract enforcement, enabling 173.3M WOZX (~$68.1m) treasury transfers and massive MPI dumps. The Sep 2019 vesting start was retroactively disclosed in Aug 2024.

Relevant Laws:

  • Malta Criminal Code, Article 308: Fraud by false pretenses (up to 7 years).
  • Italian Penal Code, Article 640: Fraud (up to 7 years).
  • EU MAR, Article 12: Disseminating false/misleading information (criminal in member states).
  • U.S. 18 U.S.C. § 1343: Wire fraud (up to 7 years).

Application: Vanetti’s exaggerated claims and concealment of material facts (AitherCO2 distress, nominal stake transfer, true private investor identity, vesting breaches) were designed to induce investment. U.S. jurisdiction is engaged via U.S.-based MPI and marketing (Times Square billboard).

2. Alleged Market Manipulation

Description: Vanetti’s hype-driven posts, participation in the unofficial WOZX Price Group, and failure to disclose material information (AitherCO2’s €319.9m tax bomb, the £510 stake transfer, true MPI identity, vesting breaches) artificially influenced WOZX price.

Examples: Same as original plus non-disclosure of the nominal stake transfer and AitherCO2’s insolvency while promoting Efforce as backed by a strong “mother company”.

Relevant Laws: Malta VFA Act Art. 34, EU MAR Art. 12(c), U.S. Rule 10b-5.

3. Alleged Aiding and Abetting Fraud

Examples: Presence at Community Gathering #6 without correcting Visetti’s €350m claim; oversight of corporate actions concealing AitherCO2’s distress and the MBM transfer.
