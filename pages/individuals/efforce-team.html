Legal Ties to Efforce

Efforce’s whitepaper (Oct 2020) and website (2019–2023) showcased a “world-class team” of 8 energy experts, allegedly drawn from AitherCO2 S.p.A. — the Italian ESCO falsely presented as Efforce’s “mother company.” This narrative of inherited expertise, offices, and clients was central to inducing hundreds of millions in retail investment in WOZX tokens.

In truth, these individuals were AitherCO2 employees or partners, with no legal or operational ties to Efforce Ltd (Malta). Malta Business Registry filings for 2019–2022 reveal Efforce had 0 employees throughout, with no salaries paid and no staff costs recorded — confirming it was a shell company that raised millions but delivered nothing.

The “Efforce Office” — June 9, 2021

On June 9, 2021, Efforce released a video showing token holders the “Efforce office.” In reality, this was AitherCO2 S.p.A.’s Milan headquarters, with an Efforce banner placed inside, showcasing employees of AitherCO2 rather than Efforce — which had no offices, no staff, and no operations reported in annual filings.

AitherCO2, previously described as the “mother company” or “parent company” of Efforce, had no legal parental relationship with Efforce and had sold its 42.5% shareholding for a nominal €510 on Dec 15, 2020 — six months before this video was filmed.

Conclusion

The Efforce “team” was a marketing illusion. Every person showcased was an AitherCO2 employee or partner with no legal or operational ties to Efforce Ltd (Malta). Official filings confirm 0 employees, €0 in salaries, and €0 in staff costs across every year of operation. The “office video” showed AitherCO2’s Milan headquarters with an Efforce banner — filmed six months after AitherCO2 had sold its stake for €510. Three AitherCO2 shareholders (Lattuada 19%, Castelli 19%, Modorati 3%) approved accounts showing massive losses while their profiles were used to sell WOZX tokens. The cumulative effect was a facade of institutional expertise designed to induce investment in what Malta filings reveal to be a shell company with cumulative losses of €342,780 and no trace of the $12.5M–$36M in token-sale proceeds.
