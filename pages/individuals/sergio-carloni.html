Sergio Carloni was the alleged CEO of both Efforce Ltd (Malta) and AitherCO2 S.p.A. (Italy) simultaneously — the token-issuing company and its purported “mother company.” This dual role placed Carloni at the nexus of every major alleged failure: the concealment of AitherCO2’s €319.9M tax liability from token holders, the nominal €510 transfer of a 42.5% stake (potentially worth €600M+) to MBM Holdings AG, and direct control over the Efforce crypto wallet keys alongside Ken Hardesty and Jacopo Vanetti.

On the Nov 18, 2023 private call, Jacopo Visetti identified U.S.-based private investors as including “One from NYC… Western US” — a description that allegedly includes Carloni. If confirmed, this makes Carloni both the CEO who approved the private placement and one of the private investors who benefited from it — an extraordinary, undisclosed conflict of interest.

Alleged Criminal Charges

1. Fraud by False Representation

Carloni allegedly made or oversaw false statements about AitherCO2’s financial position while serving as CEO of both entities:

  • “AitherCO2 turnover north of €450M” (Dec 15, 2021) — actual 2020 revenue was €27.6M per Italian filings.
  • €319.9M tax liability undisclosed — notified to AitherCO2 in 2018, provisioned minimally, not disclosed to token holders until years later.
  • Alleged U.S. private investor — Visetti (Nov 18, 2023): “One from NYC… Western US.”
  • €510 MBM transfer (Dec 15, 2020) — as dual CEO, Carloni would have had oversight of the transfer of a 42.5% stake worth potentially €600M+ for a nominal sum.

Applicable laws: Italian Penal Code Art. 640 (up to 7 years), EU MAR Art. 12 (criminal in member states), U.S. 18 U.S.C. § 1343 (wire fraud, up to 20 years).

2. Breach of Fiduciary Duty

As dual CEO, Carloni owed fiduciary duties to both entities and, by extension, to token holders who invested based on representations about AitherCO2’s backing:

  • No segregation of roles — simultaneous CEO of the entity selling tokens and the entity whose financials were used to justify the sale.
  • Wallet key control — Visetti (Jul 15, 2024): “Wallet keys: Carloni, Hardesty, Vanetti.” Direct control over treasury containing hundreds of millions in token value.
  • €510 MBM transfer — oversaw or permitted a stake transfer that potentially stripped €600M+ in value with no disclosure.

Applicable laws: Italian Civil Code Art. 2391 (director conflicts of interest), Malta Companies Act.

3. Alleged Aiding Non-Compliance with Financial Reporting

As CEO of AitherCO2, Carloni bore direct responsibility for the company’s financial disclosures. As CEO of Efforce, he bore responsibility for ensuring representations about AitherCO2 were accurate. The €319.9M tax liability was not disclosed in any Efforce communication, whitepaper, or community statement.

Applicable laws: Malta Companies Act Art. 182, EU Transparency Directive, Italian financial reporting requirements.

Alleged SEC Liability (U.S.)

If Carloni is confirmed as a U.S.-based private investor (as alleged by Visetti’s “One from NYC… Western US” description), U.S. securities jurisdiction is directly engaged:

  • Howey Test — WOZX was marketed as an investment in Efforce’s energy efficiency platform with expected returns, satisfying the SEC’s security definition.
  • Rule 10b-5 — alleged material omissions (AitherCO2 insolvency, true PI identity, vesting breaches) in connection with the purchase/sale of securities.
  • Section 5 (Securities Act 1933) — unregistered offering of securities to U.S. investors, despite the whitepaper explicitly prohibiting U.S. participation.
  • Insider trading — if Carloni traded WOZX while in possession of material non-public information about AitherCO2’s financial distress.

Relevance: A dual CEO who is also an undisclosed private investor represents one of the most fundamental conflicts of interest in securities law. The SEC has historically pursued such cases aggressively — see SEC v. Cuban (2013), SEC v. Ripple Labs (2023).

The Conflict of Interest

Carloni’s alleged dual role creates a web of conflicts that, if proven, would represent one of the most egregious governance failures in the crypto space:

  • As CEO of Efforce: responsible for ensuring token holders received accurate information about the company’s backing, including AitherCO2’s financial health.
  • As CEO of AitherCO2: had direct knowledge of the €319.9M tax liability that undermined every claim of “€250–450M revenue” made to token holders.
  • As alleged private investor: purchased tokens at $0.06–$0.08 while the public paid $0.50–$3.00+, with full knowledge of the undisclosed risks.
  • As wallet key holder: had direct ability to approve or block treasury transfers, including the $68M+ in unexplained movements.
  • As entity setup advisor: likely involved in the structuring of both Malta and Bahamas entities, as well as the MBM Holdings shell arrangement.

No disclosure of any of these overlapping roles was ever made to token holders.
