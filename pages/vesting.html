Market Dominance on Listing Day

99.2%

On December 3, 2020 — the day WOZX listed on HBTC — Efforce and its private investors controlled an estimated 99.2% of the vested token supply. Retail investors were buying into a market where the sellers held virtually all the available tokens.

Retrospective claim (Aug 2024): Efforce disclosed that vesting actually started September 23, 2019 — over a year before listing — meaning approximately 160M WOZX were already unlocked by listing day. This was never disclosed at the time. The 14-month head start gave private investors and insiders a massive, undisclosed advantage over retail buyers.

The Retroactive Disclosure (August 2024)

In August 2024 — nearly four years after listing — Efforce disclosed for the first time that the vesting start date was September 23, 2019, not December 3, 2020 (listing day) as investors had been led to believe.

This retroactive disclosure means:

  • ~160M WOZX were already unlocked by listing day — never disclosed to retail buyers.
  • The “0.27% daily” vesting rate had been running for 14 months before any retail investor could participate.
  • The on-chain data showing “breaches” based on a Dec 2020 start may technically align with a Sep 2019 start — but this start date was deliberately concealed from investors who were told vesting would begin at listing.
  • This retroactive framing appears designed to retrospectively justify transfers that were, at the time they occurred, presented to investors as impossible under the published vesting schedule.

Conclusion

The vesting schedule was the single most important investor protection in the WOZX token structure. It was the mechanism that was supposed to prevent exactly what happened: insiders dumping hundreds of millions of tokens on retail buyers. The whitepaper promised strict, scheduled releases. The community was told “NONE OF THEM HAS THE INTENTION TO SELL.” In reality, there was zero smart contract enforcement, 25.7M WOZX were moved before listing even began, and the vesting start date was retroactively disclosed four years later. The result: 610M WOZX dumped and an estimated $400M+ in retail losses. All allegations remain entirely unrebutted.
