HTX Ventures Identified as Buyer of Entire 450M WOZX Private Sale — $45M in 1 Round

According to icodrops.com/efforce, HTX Ventures (formerly Huobi Ventures) invested $45M in a single round to purchase the entire 450M WOZX private sale allocation. This is consistent with: (a) Bloomberg’s December 14, 2020 report of Huobi’s “strategic investment” in Efforce; (b) HBTC’s role as WOZX’s primary listing platform; (c) HBTC being founded by former Huobi executives; and (d) the October 2021 coordinated dump aligning precisely with HBTC’s shutdown timeline.

This identification has been alleged to Efforce and remains unrebutted. HTX Ventures has not yet been approached for a rebuttal. If HTX Ventures controlled all wallets in this analysis, the implications for coordinated market manipulation are significantly amplified.

DWF Labs & Ellipti — Market Makers, Not Institutional Investors

Pitchbook: DWF Options (now DWF Labs) and Ellipti listed alongside HTX Ventures

Pitchbook records show two additional entities associated with Efforce’s investment rounds: DWF Options (now DWF Labs) and Ellipti. It is unclear whether these participated in the private placement side or the Efforce token side — the details of the arrangement are not outlined.

What is clear, however, is that neither is a traditional institutional investor who would hold for the long term as Efforce represented to its community:

  • DWF Labs (formerly DWF Options) is a crypto market maker and trading firm, not a buy-and-hold investor. Its business model centres on providing liquidity, OTC trading, and token management — the opposite of “cornerstone investors who are strategic and long-term holding.” DWF Options also had established ties to Huobi. See DWF Labs entity profile.
  • Ellipti is a VC/market-making hybrid operating in crypto markets. Like DWF, its involvement suggests active trading and liquidity provision rather than passive long-term holding. See Ellipti entity profile.

The involvement of market makers — rather than institutional investors — is consistent with the on-chain evidence showing rapid distribution and exchange-bound outflows rather than the long-term holding that was repeatedly promised to retail token holders.

Efforce presented the Huobi relationship as a “strategic partnership” (Bloomberg, Dec 14, 2020), implying long-term alignment between Huobi/HTX and Efforce’s energy efficiency mission. The evidence suggests something quite different:

The pattern suggests a coordinated profit-extraction operation rather than a genuine strategic partnership:

  • Listed on HBTC, not HTX (Huobi): WOZX was listed on HBTC (a subsidiary exchange founded by former Huobi executives) rather than Huobi’s own T1 exchange. HBTC had weaker regulatory oversight, fewer KYC/AML controls, and money laundering allegations (Forbes, Jul 6, 2020) — ideal conditions for large-scale token distribution without scrutiny.
  • Market makers on HBTC and Bithumb: DWF Options and Ellipti — trading/market-making firms with ties to Huobi — aligned with profit-taking rather than ecosystem building.
  • HBTC closed, histories destroyed: When HBTC shut down (Oct 2021), all intra-exchange trading histories were lost. Token holders can no longer verify how their WOZX was traded within the exchange, who was on the other side of trades, or whether wash trading or coordinated liquidation occurred.
  • Investor identities kept anonymous: Throughout, Efforce refused to identify the private investors, allowing the fiction of “Asia-based institutional investors” to persist until Visetti’s private disclosure (Nov 2023) and the ICODrops/Pitchbook evidence.

In combination, this evidence suggests that the “strategic partnership” was in reality a vertically integrated arrangement: HTX Ventures bought the tokens, HBTC provided the exchange with minimal oversight, DWF/Ellipti provided market-making liquidity, and Efforce provided the celebrity-backed narrative — all while retail investors were told the private investors were long-term holders with no intention to sell.

Executive Summary

On December 15, 2019, 449.9M WOZX were transferred from the Efforce Treasury Wallet (ETW) to a single wallet: the Main Private Investor (MPI) at 0x078f4ffc522949afdc3c9ccdf794bdac159cbefb. This single allocation contradicts Andrea Castiglione’s repeated claims of multiple institutional investors (e.g., Dec 12, 2020: “450M tokens were sold to Asia-based institutional investors”).

On December 1, 2020 — two days before listing — the MPI transferred 100M WOZX to an Anchor Investor wallet. The remaining 350M WOZX were distributed across 58 outgoing transactions totalling ~$283M at transaction-time prices. The MPI’s pre-listing transfers of 17.2M WOZX (~$30M) constituted immediate vesting breaches.

Jacopo Visetti privately confirmed (Nov 18, 2023) that the 450M WOZX was sold to only two U.S.-based investors — “One investor is from NYC… the other from Western US. One of the two investors is also a regulated entity. One of the partners of one of the two investors is of Chinese descent.” This directly contradicts claims of Asia-based institutional investors and establishes clear U.S. jurisdictional nexus for SEC enforcement.

The October 2021 Coordinated Dump

On October 29, 2021, the MPI wallet distributed 186.6M WOZX (~$69.2M) across 8+ wallets in a single day at $0.3708/WOZX. This was preceded by the Anchor Investor transferring 71.2M WOZX to the likely-HBTC wallet on October 6. The timing aligned precisely with HBTC’s shutdown. Motive 1: HBTC Closure as Primary Driver

Private investors holding WOZX in HBTC-linked wallets urgently liquidated to avoid losing access to assets before HBTC’s shutdown. The likely-HBTC wallet (0x3ad5d910…) received 96.8M WOZX (~$51.9M) from both MPI and Anchor, then distributed ~90M WOZX to ~20 wallets — consistent with HBTC’s withdrawal process. Its high-volume outflows and lack of KYC/AML align with HBTC’s money laundering allegations (Forbes, July 6, 2020).

Motive 2: Pre-Organised Distribution Network

HBTC was a pre-arranged conduit for private token distribution, orchestrated by Efforce insiders and Peng Wang’s BCH Ecological Fund. Early investors sold at peak prices (~$3.15, Dec 2020) while later investors held. The October 2021 dump was a final exit as HBTC’s closure disrupted the hype, with insiders and select investors cashing out via 0x3ad5d910…. The tiered structure — where SeeleN members locked tokens to get allocations at $0.03–$0.12, only to see MPI sell above $1.50 — favoured early investors at retail’s expense.

HBTC Hypothesis & Chinese Whistleblower Context

A Chinese whistleblower (identity protected, verified by Michael Copley through private communications 2023–2024) stated that Chinese WOZX investors involved with SeeleN were required to receive their tokens within HBTC, meaning wallets like 0x3ad5d910… and 0xd5f63b76… could be HBTC-managed allocations enabling trading outside blockchain scrutiny.

Conclusion

The 450M WOZX private sale was sold to a single entity despite repeated claims of multiple institutional investors. HTX Ventures (formerly HBTC/Huobi) invested $45M in one round per icodrops.com — alleged to Efforce and unrebutted. Alongside HTX Ventures, Pitchbook records show DWF Labs and Ellipti — both market makers, not long-term institutional investors — further contradicting the “cornerstone investor” narrative. The MPI’s 58 outgoing transactions realised approximately $283M at transaction-time prices (~6.3x on a $45M investment) while retail investors were told “NONE OF THEM HAS THE INTENTION TO SELL” and the token now trades at 0.01x of the investment price. The October 29, 2021 coordinated dump of 186.6M WOZX ($69.2M) — timed precisely with HBTC’s shutdown — represents one of the most significant single-day token liquidation events in cryptocurrency history. The entire structure — HTX Ventures as buyer, HBTC as low-oversight exchange, DWF/Ellipti as market makers, HBTC closure destroying trading histories — points to a vertically integrated profit-extraction operation rather than the strategic partnership presented to retail investors. All allegations remain entirely unrebutted.
