Legal Protection

All content on this site is published responsibly and is protected under Defamation Act 2013 Section 4 (publication on matter of public interest). The publications meet the statutory requirements because:

Subject Matter of Clear Public Interest

Investor protection, alleged corporate misconduct, and matters now actively litigated in public court proceedings (Case 25CV459519). The allegations concern potential losses of $350M+ affecting thousands of retail token holders globally.

Verifiable Evidence

All statements draw from public sources including: U.S. court filings (Santa Clara County Superior Court), Milan court certificates, blockchain transaction records (Etherscan, independently verifiable), official company filings (Malta Business Registry, Bahamas Companies Registry, Italian CCIAA), and private correspondence verified for authenticity.

Responsible Journalism Standards

Sources are cited wherever possible. Allegations are clearly marked as alleged. Balanced presentation includes counter-evidence and alternative interpretations where they exist. All subjects have been invited to respond.

Fair Opportunity to Reply

Efforce and related parties have been invited to respond. Prior notice was served to Hogan Lovells on 3 May 2025. Any responses received will be published in full and unedited, both on this page and on the specific page(s) to which they relate.

Efforce Right of Reply

Efforce Ltd, its officers, directors, promoters, and legal counsel are expressly invited to provide a response to any content on this site. All replies will be published unedited and in full, both on this page and on the specific page(s) to which they relate.

This invitation extends to all named individuals and entities, including but not limited to: Steve Wozniak, Ken Hardesty, Jacopo Visetti, Jacopo Vanetti, Andrea Castiglione, Sergio Carloni, Paolo Pastore, Corrado Catania, Stefano Scozzese, Efforce Ltd (Malta), Efforce Ltd (Bahamas), AitherCO2 S.p.A., MBM Holdings AG, HTX Ventures, DWF Labs, Ellipti, and their respective legal representatives.

No evidence-based rebuttal has been received to date.

Corrections Policy

Investigatingefforce.com is committed to accuracy. If any factual error is identified:

  • Corrections will be made promptly upon verification.
  • All corrections will be clearly noted on the relevant page with the date of correction.
  • The original text and the corrected text will both be shown for transparency.
  • Material corrections will be noted on this page.

To submit a correction, email [email protected] with the specific page, the statement in question, and the evidence supporting the correction.

Evidence Standards

Content on this site is based on the following categories of evidence:

  • Court filings: U.S. court documents from Case 25CV459519 (public record).
  • Blockchain data: Ethereum transaction records verifiable on Etherscan by any person.
  • Company filings: Official accounts and registrations from Malta Business Registry, Bahamas Companies Registry, Italian Chamber of Commerce (CCIAA), Swiss commercial registry.
  • Public statements: Telegram messages, Discord messages, social media posts, press releases, and interviews — all from public channels with screenshots preserved.
  • Private communications: Verified for authenticity; sources protected where safety requires. Content attributed to named individuals has been put to those individuals for comment.
  • Third-party sources: Bloomberg, Yahoo Finance, Forbes, ICODrops, Pitchbook, CoinGecko — independently verifiable.
