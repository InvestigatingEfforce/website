Regulatory Landscape

Efforce’s corporate structure spans five jurisdictions. The following regulators have potential jurisdiction over the alleged conduct. All contact details are publicly available on each regulator’s official website.

Note on framing: This section provides factual information about regulatory authorities and their areas of jurisdiction as they relate to the Efforce investigation. Contact details are published by these regulators for the purpose of receiving public reports. Any individual who believes they have been affected by the conduct described on this site may wish to consider whether reporting to the relevant authority is appropriate for their circumstances. This is not legal advice.
🇺🇸 United States
SEC
Securities and Exchange Commission
The SEC regulates securities markets, enforces federal securities laws, and investigates fraud. The SEC’s Office of the Whistleblower offers financial awards of 10–30% of sanctions over $1M.
Jurisdiction relevant to Efforce: Unregistered securities offerings involving U.S. persons, securities fraud, market manipulation. The Howey Test determines whether WOZX qualifies as a security. U.S. nexus established via Wozniak (CA), Hardesty (CA), U.S.-based private investors, and Times Square promotion.
Key issues: Unregistered offering (Section 5), securities fraud (Section 10(b) / Rule 10b-5), Wozniak’s “shares of stock” statement, 99.2% supply control at listing, $283M PI profits
SEC Whistleblower Program → Tips, Complaints & Referrals (TCR) →
🇺🇸 United States
FINRA
Financial Industry Regulatory Authority
FINRA oversees broker-dealers and registered securities professionals in the United States. It investigates misconduct by individuals with securities industry registrations or associations.
Jurisdiction relevant to Efforce: Kenneth Hardesty — California-based, associated with Gerbsman Partners (CEO in Residence), alleged role as MPI wallet selector and treasury wallet key holder. FINRA has jurisdiction over former and current registered persons.
Key issues: Hardesty’s role in MPI selection, wallet key custodianship (Visetti Jul 2024), 4% WOZX allocation, Gerbsman Partners Dubai event promotion
FINRA Complaint Center →
🇧🇸 Bahamas
SCB
Securities Commission of The Bahamas
The SCB regulates the securities industry in The Bahamas, including digital asset businesses. Efforce Ltd (Bahamas) is the token issuer per the whitepaper.
Jurisdiction relevant to Efforce: Efforce Ltd (Bahamas) is the legal entity that issued the WOZX token according to the whitepaper. No filings since 2022, nominal capital <$50K, no registered digital asset business licence identified.
Key issues: Non-filing since 2022, opaque ownership, $12.5M–$36M token sale proceeds not reflected in any filing, potential unregistered digital asset business
SCB Official Website → Registry Search →
🇮🇹 Italy
CONSOB
Commissione Nazionale per le Società e la Borsa
CONSOB is Italy’s securities market regulator. It supervises companies, intermediaries, and market conduct. It has authority over Italian-registered entities and individuals operating in Italian markets.
Jurisdiction relevant to Efforce: AitherCO2 S.p.A. (Italian registered) — “mother company” of Efforce per Castiglione. €319.9M tax liability notified Oct 2018, €408M cumulative losses. Promoted as “$250M revenue” to WOZX investors.
Key issues: Misleading disclosures about AitherCO2 financial strength, concealment of €319.9M tax liability from token purchasers, market manipulation via false financial claims
CONSOB Complaints →
🇨🇭 Switzerland
FINMA
Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority
FINMA supervises financial institutions in Switzerland, including banks, insurance companies, and intermediaries. It investigates potential violations of Swiss financial market law.
Jurisdiction relevant to Efforce: MBM Holdings AG (Swiss registered) acquired 42.5% of Efforce for €510 on Dec 15, 2020 — twelve days after WOZX listing at $1B+ market cap. No filings since May 2021. Beneficial ownership undisclosed.
Key issues: Opaque corporate structure, no filings since 2021, €510 acquisition vs implied billion-dollar valuation, suspected layering vehicle
FINMA Reporting Irregularities →
🇲🇹 Malta
MFSA
Malta Financial Services Authority
The MFSA regulates financial services in Malta, including virtual financial assets (VFA) under the Virtual Financial Assets Act (2018). Malta is the primary operating jurisdiction per Efforce’s registered address.
Jurisdiction relevant to Efforce: Efforce Ltd (Malta C 94737) — registered office. €25K total revenue, 0 employees, €0 staff costs across all filed years (2019–2022). No VFA licence identified despite operating a token-based platform.
Key issues: 0 employees operating a claimed technology platform, no token sale proceeds in filings, potential unregistered VFA service provider, €342,780 cumulative losses with no apparent business activity
MFSA Report a Concern → VFA Framework →
🇬🇧 United Kingdom
FCA
Financial Conduct Authority
The FCA regulates financial markets and firms in the UK. It oversees crypto-asset promotions under the Financial Promotions regime (Oct 2023) and maintains the UK Warning List for unauthorised firms.
Jurisdiction relevant to Efforce: UK-based investors affected by WOZX. The FCA has jurisdiction over crypto-asset promotions targeting UK consumers. The investigation is UK-based and published under UK law (Defamation Act 2013 s.4).
Key issues: Potential unauthorised financial promotion to UK consumers, absence from FCA register, UK investor losses
FCA Report a Scam → FCA Warning List →
