Section 1

Law Enforcement Investigations & Private Investor Conduct

2:32 Visetti: “The law enforcements are… on it… this must be kept extremely confidential.”

2:58 Visetti: “We don’t have any proof because it’s like there’s none.”

3:19 Visetti: A private investigation had confirmed the PI lock-up schedule was respected.

Wider context (public evidence): Blockchain data shows the Main Private Investor wallet (0x078f…) executed 32 vesting breaches, transferring 55.3M WOZX (~$296M) between Dec 2020–Jan 2022, including 17.2M pre-listing. The ETW transferred 173.3M WOZX (~$68.1M) to 15 wallets — 160.2M remain unaccounted for.

Investor Impact: Retail investors were led to believe tokens were locked and scarce.

Legal Concern: Potential securities fraud (SEC Rule 10b-5), EU MAR Article 17, AML violations.

Section 2

Fundraising & Private Investors

7:05 Visetti: “The lead investors would cover most of the investment and then the two others would follow.”

7:29 Visetti: Private investors received a $1.2M–$1.5M marketing budget.

7:45 Visetti: “Our aim was never to… have the token on exchange… that was never the aim to sell to retail investors and actually never did.”

Wider context (public evidence): Visetti later confirmed the main investors were US-based (“One from NYC… Western US” — Discord, Jul 15, 2024), contradicting earlier claims of Asia-based institutions. Only $12M was ultimately received despite public announcements of up to $80M raised.

Investor Impact: Retail holders believed in strong institutional backing.

Legal Concern: Securities fraud (SEC Rule 10b-5), EU MAR Article 12.

Section 3

Exchange Listings & Market Makers

10:08 Visetti: “Efforce… never listed on any exchange.” (Only Huobi was listed by Efforce.)

15:43 Visetti: A private investor had appointed a market maker on Bithumb.

Wider context (public evidence): WOZX appeared on HBTC, Bithumb, and others. Blockchain data shows large dumps routed through HBTC’s hot wallet (0x3ad5d910…). Visetti separately confirmed private investors received a $1.2M–$1.5M marketing budget and that HBTC’s Times Square WOZX promotion occurred. If Efforce did not arrange these listings, the question arises: who did? This potentially raises the possibility that the Private Investor(s) played a broader role in exchange relationships and marketing execution.

Investor Impact: Undisclosed third-party control enabled coordinated selling.

Legal Concern: Securities fraud (SEC Rule 10b-5), EU MAR Article 15.

Section 4

Bithumb Delisting & Communication Failures

11:39, 18:14 Visetti: Bithumb’s delisting emails went to unofficial channels (Twitter, Gmail).

12:13 Visetti: Bithumb confirmed no contract with Efforce.

Wider context (public evidence): Bithumb’s 24-hour delisting notice was sent to two unknown Gmail addresses and an X account that was not Efforce’s official channel. This coincided with large-scale token dumps in the 24 hours prior to Bithumb posting the official delisting notice. Bithumb delisted WOZX in 2022 after unfulfilled $1.2M marketing and $50M off-take promises (Jun 23, 2022).

If the recipients of the delisting notice had advance knowledge and were able to sell aggressively before the public announcement, this potentially raises the question of whether parties with non-public access to Bithumb’s communications were able to benefit from that information.

Investor Impact: Delisting and lack of transparency eroded trust and value.

Legal Concern: Securities fraud (SEC Rule 10b-5), potential insider trading.

Section 5

Huobi & Exchange Operations

Wider context (public evidence): HBTC’s listing enabled large dumps and featured WOZX on Times Square billboards. Paolo Pastore (private message to Copley, Nov 15, 2022): “The token was pumped and dumped by private investors… sitting on millions of tokens.”

Investor Impact: False exchange promises and undisclosed private investor control inflated and then crashed the price.

Legal Concern: Securities fraud (SEC Rule 10b-5), EU MAR Article 15.
