Regulatory Framework – Swiss Stock Corporations (AG) Public Filings & Transparency (2019–2025)

Swiss Code of Obligations (OR) & Commercial Register Ordinance: All AGs must register key events (incorporation, address, directors, capital, purpose) in the Cantonal Commercial Register with mandatory publication in the Swiss Official Gazette of Commerce (SHAB/FUSC). Annual financial statements and beneficial ownership details are **not** publicly filed for small/dormant AGs.

Audit & Filing Exemptions for Small AGs: Companies below thresholds (balance sheet < CHF 20m, turnover < CHF 40m, employees < 250 for two years) can opt out of ordinary audit (limited review or full waiver with shareholder declaration). Dormant holding companies like MBM typically file only basic SHAB notices — no public accounts or consolidated group reporting required unless control thresholds trigger consolidation.

Beneficial Ownership & Related-Party Transparency: Internal register of beneficial owners (Art. 697l OR) must be maintained since 2019, but it is **private** (not public; accessible only to authorities on request). Significant participations (e.g., >10% stakes) must be notified internally, but no public disclosure for non-listed AGs. Related-party transactions (e.g., undervalued acquisitions) require proper board approval and arm's-length documentation, but are not publicly reported for small entities.

Regulatory Bodies:

  • Federal Commercial Registry Office / Cantonal Registers – SHAB publications
  • Federal Tax Administration – internal tax/AML oversight
  • State Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO) – company law compliance

Relevance to MBM Holding AG: As a small dormant AG, minimal public disclosure is permitted under Swiss law. However, the complete absence of filings after May 2020 while acquiring a material foreign stake raises questions about compliance with notification duties and arm's-length transaction rules.

Note: No public record of enforcement actions or additional filings identified against MBM Holding AG post-2020. Beneficial ownership remains non-public under the rules applicable in the relevant period.
