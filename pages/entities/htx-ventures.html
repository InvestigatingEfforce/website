Regulatory Framework – Crypto Venture Capital & Private Placement Investors (Singapore / Seychelles) (Dec 2020–Dec 2022)

Dec 2020 – 2021 (Private Placement / TGE period): HTX Ventures (Huobi Ventures) operated in a regulatory gray zone as the VC arm of Huobi Global (Seychelles-registered parent). Singapore entities subject to Payment Services Act (PSA) 2019 for any digital payment token (DPT) activities — mandatory AML/CFT (MAS Notice PSN02), customer due diligence, 5-year record-keeping, and suspicious transaction reporting. Private placements to accredited investors generally exempt from prospectus requirements under the Securities and Futures Act (SFA), but conflicts of interest and disclosure rules apply if tokens are deemed securities or if affiliated with trading venues (e.g. HBTC).

2022 (Post-sale to Justin Sun): Continued MAS oversight for Singapore operations; enhanced AML standards and preparation for Travel Rule. Dubai presence began emerging later under VARA (Dubai Law No. 4/2022), which classifies certain VC/liquidity activities as VASPs requiring licensing, capital adequacy and anti-manipulation compliance.

General Rules: AML/CFT compliance, prohibition on market manipulation (if tokens are securities), investor protection (due diligence, conflict disclosure). Offshore structures (Seychelles) subject to basic company and AML laws with 5-year record retention.

Regulatory Bodies:

  • MAS (Singapore) – PSA DPT licensing, AML/CFT, SFA for funds/securities
  • Seychelles FSA – company registration & AML oversight for parent entities
  • VARA (Dubai) – VASP framework from March 2022 (later relevance)
  • SEC (U.S.) – extraterritorial application if U.S. persons affected or tokens deemed securities (Howey Test / Rule 10b-5)

Potential U.S. SEC Relevance: Undisclosed conflicts (e.g. HBTC affiliation) and aggressive post-placement sales could trigger Rule 10b-5 liability or adviser disclosure obligations if WOZX qualifies as a security.

Note: No public record of enforcement actions, fines or licensing issues identified specifically against HTX Ventures or its affiliates for the 2020 Efforce private placement or related activities in the relevant jurisdictions.

Key Claims Directly Related to HTX Ventures

  1. Lead Investor in Private Placement: Alleged to have purchased majority or entirety of 450M WOZX tokens ($45M at $0.1/token) — no equity stake in Efforce Ltd (Malta).

  3. High Portfolio Returns vs WOZX Losses: Portfolio claims 291.35x average ROI (ICOdrops), while WOZX down 99.9% — suggests aggressive token sales at retail expense.

  5. Misrepresentation of Partnership: Efforce announced "strategic partnership" with Huobi Global (Aug 2020), but private placement led by Huobi Ventures (HTX Ventures) — could mislead on long-term support vs VC resale intent.
  6. Investor Selection Claims: Efforce communications alleged handpicking "Mostly Asia Based Cornerstone Institutional Investors who would not sell" for long-term support, but aggressive selling occurred — unrebutted.

PitchBook data tags DWF Labs and Ellipti as co-partners in the same private placement round alongside HTX Ventures. Terms of the placement and exact token share allocations remain undisclosed in public records. This raises unrebutted questions about the structure and control of the raise.

DWF Options / DWF Group & Ellipti – Alleged Co-Investors

DWF Options (part of the DWF Group, established in Switzerland in December 2018) is tagged in PitchBook as a participant in the same private placement round alongside HTX Ventures. Andrei Grachev — who would later found the public DWF Labs brand in 2022 — began actively partnering with the core DWF high-frequency trading operation in late 2018, at the same time he was CEO of Huobi Russia. He remained a partner in the DWF Group until the full shutdown and sale-back of Huobi Russia to Huobi Global in December 2020 — the exact month of the WOZX TGE. Ellipti (Seoul-based Web3 studio) is alleged to have been an anchor investor and potentially market maker on Bithumb. Visetti (Nov 18, 2023): “PI-appointed market maker on Bithumb” aligns with Ellipti's Korean focus and exchange relationships.

These statements emphasized "long-term support" and "cherry-picked institutions," potentially misleading investors about the investors' resale plans. Key Questions for Efforce Regarding Private Investors

  • Who were the specific private placement investors in the 2020 round, and what due diligence was conducted to verify their long-term commitment to Efforce's vision?
  • Why was the identity of private investors kept confidential from token holders despite repeated community requests for transparency?
  • What monitoring or enforcement mechanisms were in place to ensure private investors adhered to vesting schedules, and why were alleged breaches not publicly addressed?
  • How did Efforce reconcile claims of "long-term institutional support" with evidence of significant early selling by private investors?
  • What role did private investors play in arranging exchange listings and appointing market makers, and why was this authority delegated without disclosure?
  • How does Steve Wozniak's statement ("I see our tokens as shares of stock") align with Efforce's classification of WOZX as a utility token?
  • To what extent did Efforce cede control over key decisions (e.g., listings, market making, token distribution) to private investors, and was this arrangement disclosed?
  • In which legal entity were the $45 million private placement proceeds recorded, and why do public filings show no trace of these funds?
  • What ongoing communication or reporting existed between Efforce and private investors throughout the project's lifecycle, and why was this not shared with token holders?
  • Why was there a lack of transparency regarding private investor actions, given public assurances of "cherry-picked" partners committed to long-term success?

Conclusion on HTX Ventures

HTX Ventures is alleged to have led Efforce's $45M private placement, with funds unrecorded in Efforce accounts. Their high portfolio returns (up to 291x ROI) contrast with WOZX's 99.9% decline, suggesting aggressive selling. Frequent co-investments with OKX (23 rounds), LD Capital (16), Shima Capital (13), and ties to HBTC via OKX investment may indicate inter-related networks. Alleged Efforce misrepresentation of "strategic partnership" with Huobi Global vs VC arm, and lack of disclosure on investor intent to sell, raise questions on transparency.
