Regulatory Framework — International Business Companies (IBC) in The Bahamas (2019–2025)

International Business Companies Act 2000 (as amended): IBCs are designed as light-touch offshore vehicles. No requirement to file financial statements, annual accounts, or shareholder lists publicly. Only an annual return and fee (with late-payment penalties) is required to maintain active status. No public disclosure of beneficial owners, directors (beyond the registered agent), or activities.

Beneficial Ownership & AML: Since 2018 (AML amendments), companies maintain an internal register of beneficial owners, but this register is private and accessible only to authorities and obliged entities – not the public. Basic KYC and suspicious transaction reporting (STR) apply under the Financial Transactions Reporting Act.

Digital Assets Context: The Digital Assets and Registered Exchanges Act (DARE) 2020 regulates VASPs and exchanges. A pure token-issuance SPV like Efforce Ltd (Bahamas) falls under minimal oversight if it does not operate as an exchange or custodian. No whitepaper approval or licensing was required for the claimed “on behalf of Malta” structure.

Regulatory Bodies:

  • Registrar of Companies (Bahamas) – basic IBC registration and annual returns
  • Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) – AML/CFT supervision
  • Securities Commission of The Bahamas – if tokens deemed securities
  • Central Bank of The Bahamas – payments/fiat linkages (if any)

Relevance to Efforce Ltd (Bahamas): The IBC regime explains the complete absence of public filings for the $45M private placement, 1B WOZX minting, or any treasury movements. No public trace of the “controlling entity” relationship with Malta, no good-standing confirmation since 2022, and capital remained at $5k–$50k with late-fee penalties. This structure provided maximum privacy but zero accountability trail for token holders.

Note: No public record of enforcement actions, licensing, or filings confirming the claimed relationship with the Malta entity or the $45M proceeds has been identified.
