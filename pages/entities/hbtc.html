Regulatory Framework – Crypto Exchanges & Trade Record Retention (Singapore / Seychelles / U.S.) (Dec 2020–Dec 2022)

Dec 2020 – Mid 2021 (WOZX launch period): HBTC / BHEX operated in a regulatory gray zone. Singapore entity (BHEX.SG) subject to Payment Services Act (PSA) 2019 DPT services framework (notification or pending licence during transition; full Standard Payment Institution licence granted Aug 2022). Seychelles-registered affiliates (common for Huobi Group structures) subject to basic AML/CFT obligations with mandatory 5-year retention of customer transaction records, KYC data and trade logs.

Sept 2021 HBTC Shutdown: Closure without provision of downloadable trade-history exports raised unrebutted questions under data protection and AML record-keeping rules (Singapore PDPA, equivalent international standards). Licensed or notifying entities must preserve and be able to produce records for regulatory or user requests.

2021–2022 China Ban & Global Operations: Huobi Group (HBTC backer) restricted mainland China users; low KYC thresholds (<2 BTC/day no ID) enabled VPN access from restricted jurisdictions including the U.S., despite whitepaper prohibitions.

Regulatory Bodies:

  • MAS (Singapore) – PSA DPT licensing, AML/CFT, record-keeping
  • Seychelles FSA – basic company registration & AML oversight
  • FinCEN (U.S.) – MSB registration for money transmission
  • SEC (U.S.) – potential unregistered exchange / securities rules (Howey Test, Rule 10b-5)

Potential U.S. SEC Relevance: Times Square Nasdaq billboard marketing + weak KYC could trigger extraterritorial application of U.S. securities laws if WOZX deemed a security. Conflicts of interest (HTX Ventures investment + HBTC trading venue) require disclosure under broker-dealer / adviser rules.

Note: No public record of enforcement actions, fines or licensing revocation identified specifically against HBTC for the WOZX/Efforce period. However, mandatory AML record-retention and data-protection obligations applied across all relevant jurisdictions.

2. Efforce-Huobi Collaboration: Evidence from Tweets & PR

Despite the high profile Aug 2020 partnership with Huobi, WOZX launched exclusively on HBTC (not main Huobi platform), suggesting alleged close alignment between Huobi and HBTC for the token.

  • Leon Li (Huobi Founder): "Huobi will support Efforce growth with its best tools and resources" (PR Newswire).

Alleged Misrepresentation of HTX Ventures' Involvement

Public records suggest Efforce may have misrepresented HTX Ventures' (formerly Huobi Ventures) alleged $45 million private placement purchase of 450 million WOZX tokens (as per icodrops.com/efforce) as a broader strategic partnership with HTX (Huobi Global) itself. This could have led token holders to assume a priority listing on the main Huobi exchange and that the Tier 1 exchange was invested in the project's long-term success, rather than the VC arm acquiring tokens for potential aggressive resale on HBTC—a closely related entity funded by Huobi, using Huobi's tech stack, managed by Huobi partners, and leveraging Huobi token assets.

Alleged Conflict of Interest in HTX Ventures Token Sales

Public records and unrebutted allegations suggest a potential conflict of interest in HTX Ventures serving as Efforce's private placement partner while allegedly selling their tokens on HBTC, an affiliated exchange that subsequently closed and removed user transaction histories. Under U.S. securities laws, if WOZX tokens qualify as securities, VC firms like HTX Ventures may be subject to disclosure requirements for conflicts of interest, including affiliations with trading venues. No such disclosures appear in Efforce communications to token holders regarding HTX Ventures' sales on HBTC. Regulatory frameworks, such as SEC rules on conflicts for broker-dealers and investment advisers, emphasize transparency and mitigation of self-dealing risks—issues that remain unrebutted in this context.

Alleged Additional Investor Ties & Potential Conflicts

Public data on icodrops.com indicates OKX Ventures co-invested in 24 of HTX Ventures' portfolio projects. Allegations suggest OKX Ventures was also an initial funding investor in HBTC, which could indicate shared business interests and potential conflicts of interest given overlapping investment networks. HTX Ventures reportedly achieved an average ROI of 291.35x across investments listed on ICOdrops (their own site lists ~60x portfolio-wide), raising questions about incentives for aggressive token sales post-launch, especially in light of HBTC's sudden closure and withholding of WOZX token trading history and blockchain tracing showcasing evidence of wrongdoing.

2.2 DWF Options / DWF Group – Alleged Co-Investor & Market Maker

DWF Options (part of the DWF Group, established in Switzerland in December 2018) is tagged in PitchBook as a participant in Efforce’s 2020 private placement round alongside HTX Ventures. Andrei Grachev — who would later found the public DWF Labs brand in 2022 — began actively partnering with the core DWF high-frequency trading operation in late 2018, at the same time he was CEO of Huobi Russia. He remained a partner in the DWF Group until the full shutdown and sale-back of Huobi Russia to Huobi Global in December 2020 — the exact month of the WOZX TGE.

This documented timeline overlap raises unrebutted questions about potential undisclosed HTX ecosystem ties and the DWF Group’s alleged role in liquidity provision or market-making on HBTC (and related platforms) during the WOZX launch and subsequent dumps.
