Aither.com Legal Department — February 13, 2026

Dear Sirs,

We have become aware of your statements referring to AitherCO2 S.p.A. and linking those allegations to Aither.com.

For the avoidance of doubt, Aither.com is not the same company, not a legal successor, and not a continuation of AitherCO2 S.p.A. Aither.com is an entity with different shareholders, management, corporate governance, and legal structure.

While Aither.com acquired certain business units from AitherCO2 S.p.A. in the past, this does not imply any assumption of liabilities, representation, or corporate continuity.

We understand that your assumption may be based on the fact that certain former AitherCO2 personnel, including one of its founders, later joined Aither.com. However, the movement of individuals between companies does not create corporate continuity or transfer liabilities, ownership, or legal identity.

Your public statements suggesting otherwise are factually incorrect and may mislead the public. We respectfully request that you correct or clarify this point in your publications.

Furthermore, the use of a confusingly similar logo and branding may constitute trademark infringement and passing off, as it may mislead the public into believing there is an affiliation with Aither.com.

For clarity, Aither.com is not affiliated with your website and does not endorse or support its content.

We therefore request that you cease using such branding elements immediately and confirm in writing that they have been removed.

Additionally, any suggestion that Hogan Lovells represents Aither.com is incorrect.

Kind regards,
Legal & Compliance
Aither.com

Regulatory Framework – Swiss Stock Corporations (AG) Public Filings & Transparency (2019–2025)

Swiss Code of Obligations (OR) & Commercial Register Ordinance: All AGs must register key events (incorporation, name/seat changes, directors, capital increases, purpose amendments) in the Cantonal Commercial Register with mandatory publication in the Swiss Official Gazette of Commerce (SHAB/FUSC). These are the primary public disclosure mechanism.

Audit & Financial Reporting: Small/dormant AGs (below thresholds: balance sheet < CHF 20m, turnover < CHF 40m, employees < 250 for two consecutive years) can opt out of ordinary audit via shareholder declaration. No public filing of annual accounts or consolidated group reports is required unless the company exceeds thresholds or is listed.

Beneficial Ownership & Related-Party Transparency: Internal register of beneficial owners (Art. 697l OR) must be maintained since 2019, but it is **private** (accessible only to authorities). Significant participations (>10%) and related-party transactions require internal documentation but are not publicly disclosed for non-listed AGs. No mandatory public disclosure of parent/subsidiary relationships or continuity of business units unless they trigger consolidation rules (rare for small AGs).

Regulatory Bodies:

  • Federal Commercial Registry Office / Cantonal Registers – SHAB publications
  • Federal Tax Administration – internal tax/AML oversight
  • State Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO) – company law compliance

Relevance to Aither Group AG: Multiple SHAB publications exist for name/seat/capital/director changes, but no public disclosure of beneficial owners, consolidated accounts, or explicit links to AitherCO2 S.p.A. or Efforce. The entity’s evolution from emissions trading to broader commodities trading is visible, but continuity/liability issues remain non-public under Swiss rules applicable in the period.

Note: No public record of enforcement actions or additional filings identified against Aither Group AG. Beneficial ownership and group relationships remain non-public under the rules applicable in the relevant period.
