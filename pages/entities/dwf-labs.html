Regulatory Framework — DWF Labs & Precursor Entities (2020–2022)

Pre-2022 (2020–early 2022): Activities conducted through precursor entities Digital Wave Finance (Swiss HFT firm) and VRM.trade (Latvian trading entity) plus Singapore structures. Light regulation focused on AML/CFT.

Switzerland (Digital Wave Finance): Self-regulated under VQF for asset management; FINMA AML oversight. No specific crypto market-maker license required.

Latvia (VRM.trade): VASP registration under AML law (from 2020) with State Revenue Service — KYC, STR, record-keeping required.

Singapore: MAS Payment Services Act — registration or exemption for crypto trading/liquidity services; AML compliance mandatory.

2022 Onwards (DWF Labs Dubai): Operated under DMCC free-zone license with VARA oversight. Full VARA VASP framework (Law No. 4/2022) requires licensing, ISMS, Travel Rule, capital requirements, and governance for market making.

General Rules: AML/CFT (FATF-aligned), prohibition on market manipulation, investor protection (asset segregation, disclosures). “DWF Options” was the PitchBook designation for the investment/trading vehicle used in the 2020 Efforce placement.

Regulatory Bodies:

  • VARA (Dubai) — Primary VASP supervisor from 2022
  • FINMA / VQF (Switzerland) — Pre-2022 oversight
  • MAS (Singapore) — Payment Services Act compliance

Andrei Grachev — Managing Partner & Founder

Andrei Grachev is the Managing Partner and founder of DWF Labs (established 2022). He previously served as CEO of Huobi Russia. His alleged involvement in the Efforce matter is strictly external through DWF Labs / DWF Group entities.

Important Clarification: Andrei Grachev has never been an employee, director, officer, advisor or insider of Efforce Ltd, AitherCO2, MBM Holding or any related entities. Any alleged connection is limited to DWF Labs acting as an external investor / liquidity provider.

Alleged Role via DWF Labs

  • PitchBook records DWF (under Grachev) as participant in the Efforce private placement round.
  • Grachev’s background as ex-Huobi Russia CEO has led to questions about potential links to the HTX ecosystem during the 2020 Efforce TGE and HBTC listing period.
  • He remained a partner in the DWF Group until the full shutdown and sale-back of Huobi Russia to Huobi Global in December 2020 — the exact month of the WOZX TGE.
