Regulatory Framework — Crypto Token Issuers in Malta (Dec 2020–Dec 2022)

Virtual Financial Assets Act (VFAA) 2018 (fully operational from 2019): Malta was the first EU jurisdiction with a dedicated crypto framework. Any Maltese company issuing tokens classified as Virtual Financial Assets (VFAs) must appoint a licensed VFA Agent, submit a whitepaper for MFSA notification/approval, and comply with full AML/CFT rules (FATF standards, 5-year record-keeping, suspicious transaction reporting).

Companies Act & Listing Rules: Token issuers must maintain proper accounting records and file annual accounts with the Malta Business Registry. Private placements are generally exempt from full prospectus requirements, but misleading statements about corporate structure, funds raised, or relationships are prohibited. Dual-entity structures (Malta parent + offshore issuance SPV) require clear disclosure of control and fund flows in filings.

Malta / Bahamas structure: The December 2020 whitepaper claimed EFFORCE Limited (Bahamas) was formed “for the purpose of issuing EFFORCE tokens on behalf of its controlling entity EFFORCE Ltd, a Malta company.” However, exhaustive checks of the Malta Business Registry and Bahamas Corporate Registry show zero public filings, ownership records, consolidated accounts or fund-flow evidence linking the two entities. The $45 million private placement proceeds appear in neither entity’s public records.

Regulatory Bodies:

  • MFSA – VFAA licensing, whitepaper oversight, enforcement
  • FIU Malta – AML/CFT supervision & suspicious transaction reporting
  • Malta Business Registry (MBR) – company filings & accounts
  • SEC (U.S.) – extraterritorial reach if U.S. persons or WOZX deemed a security (Howey Test / Rule 10b-5)

Potential U.S. SEC Relevance: Misleading statements about corporate structure, undisclosed dual-entity arrangement, or unrecorded funds could trigger liability if WOZX meets the Howey Test.

Note: No public record of MFSA VFA licence, whitepaper approval or enforcement action identified against Efforce Ltd (Malta) or its claimed Bahamas issuance SPV. The claimed “controlling entity” relationship has no supporting public registry evidence in Malta or the Bahamas.
