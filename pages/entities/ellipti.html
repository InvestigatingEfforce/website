Regulatory Framework — Market Makers in South Korea (2020–2022)

Pre-2021 (2020): Crypto largely unregulated; market makers on exchanges like Bithumb subject to exchange rules and general AML obligations. No specific market-maker license.

March 2020: Specific Financial Information Act (SFIA) amended — classified VASPs (including potential market makers if handling virtual assets). AML/CFT obligations introduced; grace period until Sep 2021.

March 2021: SFIA effective — VASPs must register with FIU (under FSC). Requirements: ISMS certification, real-name bank accounts, CDD, STR, record-keeping.

March 2022: Travel Rule implemented — transfers > KRW 1M require originator/beneficiary info.

General Rules: AML/CFT compliance, prohibition on manipulation (Capital Markets Act), investor protection (asset segregation, disclosures). No dedicated market-maker license; oversight via exchanges.

Regulatory Bodies:

  • Financial Services Commission (FSC) — overall policy & guidelines
  • Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) — VASP registration & AML/CFT supervision
  • Financial Supervisory Service (FSS) — inspections & enforcement
  • Korea Fair Trade Commission (KFTC) — unfair practices

Potential U.S. SEC Relevance: If activities touched U.S. persons/markets or tokens deemed securities (Howey test), SEC rules (Rule 10b-5, broker-dealer registration) could apply extraterritorially. No public SEC enforcement against Ellipti during 2020–2022.

Note: No public record of enforcement actions against Ellipti under these frameworks.
