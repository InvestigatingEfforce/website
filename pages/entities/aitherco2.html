AitherCO2 S.p.A (Italy) was presented as Efforce's "mother company" with strong financial health, €250–450 million annual revenue, 40+ employees, and thousands of clients. However, as of December 2025, public filings reveal undisclosed €319.9 million tax liability (notified 2018), €408.2 million loss in 2020, negative equity, and going-concern warnings. Regulatory Framework – Italian S.p.A. Public Filings & Transparency (2018–2025)

Italian Civil Code (Codice Civile) Art. 2325–2451 & Legislative Decree 58/1998 (TUF): All S.p.A. must file key events (incorporation, capital changes, directors, auditors, mergers) with the Registro delle Imprese (Chamber of Commerce). Filings are public and include annual accounts (bilancio).

Annual Accounts & Audit: Bilancio must be approved by shareholders and filed within 30 days (Art. 2435). Statutory audit is mandatory for S.p.A. exceeding thresholds (revenue > €50m, assets > €25m, employees > 500 for two consecutive years) or if listed. Smaller S.p.A. can use a collegio sindacale or opt for external auditor. Going-concern warnings and tax provisions must be disclosed in notes.

Beneficial Ownership & Related-Party Transparency: Since 2017 (D.Lgs 90/2017, 4AMLD) and strengthened in 2023 (D.Lgs 231/2023), companies maintain an internal register of beneficial owners (titolare effettivo). This register is **not publicly accessible** (only to authorities and obliged entities). Significant shareholdings (>10%) and related-party transactions must be disclosed in the notes to the accounts (IAS 24 or Italian GAAP), but full group structures and ultimate beneficial owners remain private for unlisted S.p.A.

Regulatory Bodies:

  • Camera di Commercio / Registro delle Imprese – public filings & SHAB-equivalent publications
  • Agenzia delle Entrate – tax returns and debt enforcement
  • Guardia di Finanza – tax fraud and AML investigations
  • CONSOB – if securities offered (not applicable here)

Relevance to AitherCO2 S.p.A.: Annual bilanci are publicly available (showing revenue, losses, tax provisions, employees), but beneficial ownership register, full group relationships, and internal restructuring details are not public. No mandatory disclosure of links to Swiss entities or Efforce beyond what appears in the notes. The €319.9M tax liability is visible in 2020 accounts as a provision, but earlier notifications and appeals were internal until disclosed in filings.

Note: No public record of enforcement actions beyond standard tax assessments has been identified in open sources.
