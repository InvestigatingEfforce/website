Valuation Methodology Note

A number of significant transactions occurred prior to WOZX’s initial listing on Dec 3, 2020. Pre-listing transfers are valued at the Week 1 average price of $1.74 rather than the private sale price (~$0.06–$0.10). This methodology is used because: (a) these tokens were transferred from the treasury wallet, not the private sale allocation; (b) they entered circulation at or near listing when the market price was established; and (c) the private sale price reflects a discount for risk/lockup that these treasury tokens did not carry.

If the alternative private sale valuation ($0.10) were applied, the total unaccounted value would be significantly lower (~$16M vs $68M). Both figures represent material amounts that were never disclosed in any filing. The $68M figure is used throughout this analysis as the more conservative reflection of market impact. This methodology is subject to review as comprehensive tracing is completed.

Executive Summary

The Efforce Treasury Wallet (ETW) held 550M WOZX post-private placement (Dec 15, 2019), allocated across three budgets: Ecosystem & Consultants (150M), Project Incentives/Mining (200M), and Team (200M). Between November 2020 and March 2023, 54 transactions transferred 173,317,834 WOZX to 15 recipient wallets.

Of this total, only 13,146,991 WOZX ($2.6M) has been accounted for — allocated to staking and community competitions. The remaining 160,170,843 WOZX ($68.1M) is entirely unaccounted for, with no explanations, no transparency, and no disclosure in any filing of either Efforce Ltd (Malta) or Efforce Ltd (Bahamas).

The first transfer occurred on November 29, 2020 — four days before listing — constituting a vesting breach. The largest single unaccounted transfer was 75M WOZX to Wallet 3 (Oct 25, 2022), later confirmed by Paolo Pastore as having been “sold to U.S. VC” and found to hold $49M in alternative cryptocurrencies (Feb 2025).

Background & Token Structure

The ETW held 550M of the 1 billion WOZX total supply, divided as follows:

  • Ecosystem & Consultants: 150M WOZX (15%) — “no more than 1.5% released monthly” (whitepaper ambiguity: 1.5% of budget = 2.25M/month vs. 1.5% of total supply = 15M/month; never clarified).
  • Project Incentives/Mining: 200M WOZX (20%) — “no more than 5% released annually,” post-platform launch. The platform never launched, so these tokens should not have been released.
  • Team: 200M WOZX (20%) — “no more than 1% released annually” over 20 years (corrected to 10M/year).

Critical failures: Tokens were held in a single wallet with no budget segregation. No smart contract enforcement. No transaction logs or balance disclosures were ever provided to token holders. Efforce Ltd (Malta) accounts omit all crypto transactions. Efforce Ltd (Bahamas) filed no accounts at all.

Wallet key holders (confirmed by Visetti, Jul 15, 2024): Sergio Carloni, Ken Hardesty, Jacopo Vanetti.

Conclusion

The Efforce Treasury Wallet analysis reveals $68.1M in unaccounted token transfers across 15 recipient wallets. Only 7.6% of transferred tokens (13.1M WOZX) have any identified purpose. The remaining 92.4% (160.2M WOZX) vanished without explanation, without disclosure in any filing, and without any transparency to the thousands of retail token holders whose investments were being diluted. The largest unaccounted wallet was confirmed by the Managing Director to have been “sold to U.S. VC” and now holds $49M in alternative cryptocurrencies. The wallet key holders — Carloni, Hardesty, and Vanetti — bear direct responsibility for every transaction. All allegations remain entirely unrebutted.
