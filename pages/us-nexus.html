Overview

Efforce Ltd (Malta) and its purported subsidiary Efforce Ltd (Bahamas) issued the WOZX cryptocurrency token in 2020, marketed as a utility token for energy efficiency investments. The project was backed by Steve Wozniak (U.S. citizen, California, 11% shareholder) and Ken Hardesty (U.S. citizen, California, 4% shareholder, CEO in Residence at Gerbsman Partners), and raised $12.5–$36 million through a private token sale of 450 million WOZX to an alleged U.S.-based Main Private Investor (MPI), with 100 million WOZX transferred to an affiliated Anchor Investor — despite the whitepaper explicitly prohibiting U.S. token holders.

The Efforce Treasury Wallet transferred 173,317,834 WOZX ($68.1M) to 15 recipient wallets from November 2020 to March 2023, with 160,170,843 WOZX ($68.1M) unaccounted. The MPI realised approximately $283M from 58 outgoing transactions. Collectively, these actions allegedly contributed to ~$350 million in losses for retail token holders globally, including U.S. investors, due to alleged fraud, misrepresentation, market manipulation, and non-delivery of promised platform functionality.

This memorandum establishes the U.S. nexus for legal action under U.S. securities laws, incorporating blockchain transaction data, vesting breaches, whistleblower insights, and insider statements evidencing misconduct.

The whitepaper ban appears to have been a legal fig leaf — a disclaimer included to create the appearance of regulatory compliance while every substantive action contradicted it.

Howey Test Analysis — WOZX as a Security

Under SEC v. W.J. Howey Co. (1946), an “investment contract” exists where there is: (1) an investment of money, (2) in a common enterprise, (3) with an expectation of profits, (4) derived from the efforts of others. WOZX satisfies all four prongs:

1. Investment of Money

$12.5–$36M private sale. Retail purchases on HBTC, Bithumb, and other exchanges. Wozniak: “I see owning WOZX as shares of stock.”

2. Common Enterprise

Horizontal commonality: all token holders’ fortunes tied to Efforce platform success. Tokens pooled via single treasury wallet. Promoters pooled proceeds.

3. Expectation of Profits

Whitepaper promised returns from energy savings. Marketing emphasised token value appreciation. SeeleN promoted $10 price target. Wozniak’s “shares of stock” = investment framing.

4. Efforts of Others

Platform development solely by Efforce/AitherCO2. Token holders had no control over treasury, vesting, or platform delivery. Wallet keys held by Carloni, Hardesty, Vanetti — not token holders.

If WOZX qualifies as a security, its offer and sale without SEC registration violates Section 5 of the Securities Act of 1933. Misrepresentations in connection with its sale violate Section 10(b) and Rule 10b-5 of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934.

Class Action Opportunity

  • Numerosity: Thousands of retail token holders affected globally, including U.S. persons who purchased on HBTC without KYC enforcement.
  • Commonality: All class members were subject to the same misrepresentations (vesting schedules, investor identity, platform delivery, AitherCO2 financial health).
  • Typicality: Named plaintiffs’ claims arise from the same course of conduct as all class members.
  • Adequacy: Coordination via investigatingefforce.com; California lawsuit already filed (25CV459519).

Estimated recovery: $350M+ — representing MPI profits ($283M), ETW unaccounted transfers ($68.1M), and additional claims against Efforce, its directors, and related entities.

The U.S. nexus for Efforce/WOZX enforcement is not speculative — it is structural and confirmed. Two U.S. citizens held 15% of the company as shareholders and served as directors.
